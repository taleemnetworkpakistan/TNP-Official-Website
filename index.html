Who We Are

Our Vision & Mission

TNP is a voluntary, nation-building project under the Nazriya Pakistan Council (Trust) at Aiwan-i-Quaid, Islamabad — dedicated to universal literacy for Pakistani boys and girls.

📖

Vision

Knowledge is bliss. It makes a person complete. To acquire knowledge and wisdom, we seek education. TNP strives to bring literacy to every Pakistani child — boys and girls alike — so they may know their full potential.

🎯

Mission

TNP envisages making a valuable contribution to Pakistan's all-important literacy drive. Through the philanthropic public, we aim to find easy, affordable ways to enrol out-of-school children — providing quality education and comprehensive personality development through Taleem o Tarbiyat & Tazkiyya.

The Concept

How TNP Works

TNP's first project establishes two dedicated model Non-formal Primary Classes with an Accelerated Learning Program (ALP) on existing school premises, starting in the suburban towns of Meharbadi, Islamabad Capital Territory.

A 2023 JICA survey confirmed that large numbers of primary-age children — boys and girls — remain out of school across almost all union councils of ICT. TNP is built to address exactly this gap.

Non-Formal Primary Classes

Two model ALP classes held on running school premises — removing infrastructure barriers for out-of-school children.

JICA Partnership

Planned partly as a collaborative arrangement with the Japan International Cooperation Agency, grounding the program in rigorous field data.

No Bureaucratic Hurdles

TNP intentionally keeps its implementation model simple — empowering philanthropists to act directly without red tape.

Short-Term, Scalable Projects

Each program cycle is designed as a short-term project that can be replicated and scaled across Pakistan's union councils.

What We Teach

Program Objectives

Every TNP classroom delivers a holistic curriculum that goes beyond literacy — nurturing faith, character, and life skills alongside core academics.

🕌

Islamic Studies — Grounding children in religious knowledge and values as a foundation for ethical life.

🗣️

Urdu & English — First and second language skills: listening, reading with comprehension, speaking, and writing.

🌱

Character Building — Societal values, ethics, norms, and healthy lifestyle habits for well-rounded personality development.

🔢

Numeracy & Mathematics — Core numeracy skills and basic mathematics for practical life and further education.

🔬

General Science — Age-appropriate science concepts to build curiosity and foundational scientific thinking.

🌍

General Knowledge — Awareness of Pakistan, the world, and civic life to create informed young citizens.

🎓

FREE Quality Education — No child is turned away. TNP provides education free of cost to poor and disadvantaged children.

🏥

FREE Annual Medical Check-Up — Ensuring every enrolled child's health is monitored and supported throughout the year.

National Context

Pakistan's Education Policy

Pakistan's Constitution guarantees education as a fundamental right. TNP's work directly supports the national policy goals of access, quality, and equality.

Single National Curriculum (SNC) to ensure uniform academic standards across all schools.

100% enrollment at primary and secondary levels — eliminating gender and regional disparities.

Expansion of technical and vocational training programs linked to employment and economic growth.

Promotion of girls' education and integration of digital learning systems in schools nationwide.

Strengthening teacher training, modernizing infrastructure, and expanding science-based learning.

Alarming Reality

The Stakes Are High

When children remain outside the school system and grow up illiterate, the consequences ripple through all of society. Without education to guide them, these young people become vulnerable to exploitation, extremism, and a life of poverty. The darkness of ignorance does not stay contained — it spreads.

The Taleem Network Pakistan exists precisely to break this cycle. With every conscious citizen's cooperation, we can banish illiteracy and mould future generations into educated, enlightened, and prosperous members of the global community.

It is a gigantic task — but not Mission Impossible.

⚠ Without education, children risk...

  • Falling prey to anti-social elements and extremism
  • Drug addiction and criminal pathways
  • Permanent poverty and menial labour
  • Exclusion from civic and national life
  • A bleak and dangerous future for all of society
Get Involved

Action Plan

TNP's action plan is designed for simplicity. No bureaucracy. No red tape. Just ordinary Pakistanis choosing to change a child's fate.

1

Identify a child in your area who is of primary school age but not attending school due to poverty or lack of support.

2

Choose a school — any affordable school in your vicinity, from Rs. 50/month to Rs. 500/month. The goal is enrolment, not prestige.

3

Sponsor their education — cover fees, stationery, or uniforms. Any amount of support can change a child's trajectory.

4

Watch them flourish — the day that child excels and serves society will be your greatest moment of pride and elation.

Hold the Hand of One Child

This is our fervent appeal to the well-to-do brothers and sisters of this beloved country: come forward and get one — or more — child admitted to a school. The primary aim is not costly institutions. The primary aim is to bring deserving children within the boundary walls of a school.
